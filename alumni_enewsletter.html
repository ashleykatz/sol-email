A MESSAGE FROM THE DEAN


This past year has been an outstanding one for Liberty Law, and we thank you for being an important part of it. In August, we welcomed 77 new 1Ls and two transfer students, our largest class in several years. We celebrated the successes of our inaugural class at their 10-year reunion in September and reconnected with numerous other alumni at Homecoming in October and throughout the year.

Our bar passage continues to soar! The Class of 2017 conquered the July 2017 bar exam in 18 jurisdictions with a staggering 88% pass rate. They earned a 100% pass rate in 13 jurisdictions, and 84% in Virginia.

We launched several new initiatives in 2017 including: the Center for Law & Government, a clinic in conjunction with Virginia Legal Aid Society, the country’s first JD/CFP program, Consitution Week, and advisory boards for the Center for Prosecution and the Wealth Management and Financial Planning program to mention just a few.

Our advocacy teams continue to enjoy tremendous success. Most recently, our Moot Court team swept the Notre Dame National Appellate Advocacy Tournament for Religious Freedom, claiming the championship title and winning honors for Best Brief and Best Oralist (the first time Liberty has swept all three awards in the same competition). Additionally, our Arbitration team qualified for the ABA National Championships and traveled to Chicago where they advanced all the way to the seminfinals.

Finally, as we prepare for another great year, I’m excited to welcome three new faces to our staff who will help us continue to grow and excel. I invite you to learn more about them in the bios below. I am confident that great things lie ahead for Liberty Law as we continue to train Champions for Christ and champions of the law.

God bless,
B. Keith Faulkner
Dean

CASA PARTNERSHIP
This year, we were excited to once again be the presenting sponsor of the CASA Superhero Run and to host a reception for our fellow event sponsors. We are proud to partner with these organizations in our support of CASA of Central Virginia. This organization, which advocates for abused and neglected children in the greater Lynchburg area, directly aligns with our belief that the practice of law is a calling and a means to make a difference in the lives of others. We thank CASA for all they do for the community, and we thank our fellow sponsors for their support of this important mission.
NEW DAY, NEW FACES
Timothy Spaulding, Associate Dean for External Relations
Tim joined us on Oct. 16 to oversee the Center for Career and Professional Development, development, and public relations. His background impressively encompasses all three of these areas. His private practice in a medium-sized firm, his time as a solo practitioner, and his work as CEO and chief development officer for a local nonprofit have prepared him for this complex and challenging assignment.

Tim is a graduate of Regent School of Law. He holds a B.S. in Organizational Management from Roberts Wesleyan College and a diploma in Bible and Missions from Elim Bible Institute and College. He has held adjunct appointments in Liberty's Schools of Business, Education, and Law.
J. Bryan Boyd, Associate Dean for Administration and Student Development
Bryan joined us on Oct. 16 as the associate dean for administration and student development. Most recently, he served as the Clerk of the Supreme Court of North Carolina as the 16th person to hold the office since the establishment of the court in 1818-19.

Prior to that appointment, he served as an assistant professor of law at Campbell Law School for 13 years. In his time at Campbell, he served in a variety of roles, including director of the professional skills program, and as associate dean for academic affairs. As a professor, he received four separate teaching awards, including the distinction of being recognized as the most outstanding professor of the year on three separate occasions.

He has taught a variety of courses on the topics of legal research and writing, appellate and trial advocacy, criminal law, and criminal procedure.
Lisa M. Lucena, Associate Dean for Admissions and Financial Aid
In December, Lisa joined us all the way from Miami, Fla. She is a St. Thomas Law graduate and holds a M.S. degree in Marriage and Family Counseling from St. Thomas University as well a B.S. in Criminology and Criminal Justice from Florida State University.
